Welcome

/'Incipit/

Here begins...

The word 'incipit' derives from Latin, and means 'here begins'.

An 'incipit' refers to the beginning of any work - be it a book, song, poem, prayer, piece of music, or, in the the modern world of computer science, an encryption code.

I chose to name my practice 'Incipit Therapy', because it carries the auspicious meaning that we have the potential to transform ourselves and to start a new life at any point.

The therapist can facilitate this process of transformation and help the client to start a new path in his or her life. A path towards improved self-awareness and self-confidence, better communication, better management of difficult situations, the ability to identify and break unhealthy habits, which in turn will lead to a healthier, more fulfilling and empowered life.

Therapeutic Approach

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

My therapeutic approach incorporates traditional CBT modalities and 'third wave CBT', in particular: Mindfulness based CBT, Compassion Focussed Therapy, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy and Solution Focussed Therapy.

The term CBT identifies a family of interventions with the most extensive empirical support relating to their effectiveness.

Traditional CBT is time limited, goal oriented and collaborative, as therapist and client work together in order to achieve their therapeutic goals. It involves helping the client to identify and challenge unhealthy cognitive behavioural patterns, and find healthier and more effective ways to cope with problems.

Third wave CBT introduced new models and interventions to increase the effectiveness of traditional CBT, by introducing a more holistic approach to health and wellbeing, with the aim of not only reducing the symptoms, but also building more resilience, accessing inner resources, managing stress, achieving emotional balance, and ultimately experiencing a fuller ad more satisfying life.
